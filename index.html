The Earthquake Alert Project

Welcome to our school science fair! My name is Yahyobek, and together with my team, we built a device that helps people know when an earthquake is coming. Our goal is to use smart technology to make homes and schools safer for everyone.

The Science of Shaking

An earthquake happens when the Earth's surface moves very suddenly. This is caused by giant rocks deep underground that slide past each other. When they move, they send out waves of energy that make the ground shake. Our device is designed to find these waves quickly so we can warn people to be careful.

[Image of the seismic waves of an earthquake]

How We Measure Earthquakes

Scientists use a special tool called the Richter Scale to measure how strong an earthquake is. A small number like 2.0 means the shake is very tiny and most people won't feel it. But a big number like 7.0 or 8.0 means the earthquake is very powerful and dangerous. Our project helps people stay safe during the big ones by giving them a fast warning sound.

Project Materials

Main Brain

We used a small electronic board that acts as the brain. It reads information from our sensor and tells the alarm when to start making noise.

Vibration Sensor

This part is like a digital ear. It stays very quiet until it feels a vibration, and then it sends a signal to the brain immediately.

Our Test Results

During our science project, we tested the device 50 times! Every time we shook the table, the alarm went off in less than one second. This proves that our device is very fast and reliable. We also found that the alarm is loud enough to be heard from three rooms away, which is perfect for a big house.

Problems We Solved

Building this project was not always easy. In the beginning, the alarm was too sensitive—it would go off even if a small cat walked past it! We had to work together to change the code and make sure it only makes a sound for real shaking. We learned that engineering is all about fixing mistakes until things work perfectly.

Our Ideas for Next Year

Phone Alerts

Next year, we want to make the device send a message to your phone. This way, you can know about the earthquake even if you are not at home.

Solar Power

We want to add solar panels so the device can work even if the electricity goes out during a big storm or earthquake.

What To Do In Danger

Drop

Get down on the floor immediately.

Cover

Hide under a strong table or desk.

Hold On

Stay there until the shaking stops.
